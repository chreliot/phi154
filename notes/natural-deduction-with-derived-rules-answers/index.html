Natural Deduction with Derived Rules Answers

(Nov 25)

A sheet of proof-solutions for the exercise on TFL natural deduction with derived rules are → here.

One important caveat: these are only the solutions I happened to see first, and which seemed to me at least as efficient as other solutions I immediately saw. There are always many other solutions, and especially many other legitimate ways of ordering inferences. So, your answer that’s quite unlike what I’ve done isn’t necessarily wrong! You might consider checking it with the forall x proof checker.

Also, the first half of the questions are generally more difficult than the second half. And as I wrote out proofs for all of them, one of them, #14, seemed significantly more challenging than the others, at least with the forall x rule set.1 So, unlike the others, that is a more difficult one than I will put on your second test — at least in terms of my slightly subjective estimation of what’s difficult!

  1. It’s not more difficult if your rule set includes substitution rules for conditionals, turning on the equivalences between, first, “¬(P→Q)” and “P∧¬Q” and then also between “P→Q” and “¬P∨Q.” But the forall x rule set doesn’t allow those substitutions. ↩︎
