Variant Rule Names

(Nov 9)

In the Basic deduction rules for TFL (summarized in textbook Appendix C, section 3.4), the names of the negation rules are a little confusing. I’m not the first instructor using this textbok to point this out. So, I offer you the following alternatives. In a natural deduction proof, in your justification column,

  1. you may optionally invoke the contradiction rule (¬E) by writing “contradiction” or “contra.” instead;
  2. because ¬I and IP are symmetrical rules1, but not named symmetrically, you may optionally invoke ¬I by calling it “IP”

In other words, I won’t mark those two alternatives wrong on homework or tests. Moreover, I have allowed that you may abbreviate continuous sequences like “1, 2–3, 4–7” by writing “1–7.”

The one limitation of getting used to these alternative names is that they won’t work in the natural deduction proof checker. However, the proof checker lists all the rules in a column on the right, so you could always look up the formal name there.

  1. These two rules are each ways of adding or subtracting a negation from a sentence by assuming the opposite sentence and deriving a contradiction. If you’re trying to prove ¬P, you can assume P (the opposite), derive a contradiction, and conclude ¬P. If you’re trying to prove P, you can assume ¬P (the opposite), derive a contradiction and conclude P. Since these work the same way, I am willing to call them both “IP” for “Indirect Proof.” ↩︎
