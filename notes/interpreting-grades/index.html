Interpreting Grades

This note about grading and interpreting grades just adds some informative detail to what the course syllabus already says about grading, and it shows how grades are calculated for any particular assessment.

4-point scale

Grades in this course are assigned and calculated using Hofstra’s 4-point scale (described here in the Hofstra Bulletin). Following the Bulletin, the alphabetical grades, including plus (+) and minus (–), have the following 4-point scale values (and vice versa):

letter 4pt Student’s academic performance …
A 4.0 “was of honors level.”
A– 3.7
B+ 3.3
B 3.0 “was distinctly above that required by the course.”
B– 2.7
C+ 2.3
C 2.0 “achieved the objectives of the course.”
C– 1.7
D+ 1.3
D 1.0 “was less than required for major or minor credit but … sufficient for … full degree credit.”
F 0 “failed to satisfy the objectives of the course.”

Test grades

Each answer on tests is scored on a 4-point scale. The overall, calculated score you see on your test is then all the answers’ scores added together. For example, if there were six test questions, then the highest possible grade would be (6 questions x 4 possible points per question =) 24 points. Your overall score will normally appear on the inside back cover of your test’s blue book, or at the top, if I’m returning your test electronically.

To convert this score to a grade on a 4-point scale, divide your overall score by the number of questions. For example, if a student got an overall score of 15/24 on a 6-question test, their 4-point grade would be (15 points divided by 6 questions), which is 2.5. On the 4-point scale (see above), 2.5 is equivalent to a C+.

Accordingly, on this system, your overall grade on the test is just the average of the scores you earned on each question, or equivalently, the average points per question.

Overall course grades

Overall course grades will be calculated using the following percentages specified in the syllabus:

Component Fraction of total
Engagement 40%
First Test 30%
Second Test 30%

Final grades will be calculated on a 4-point scale and then converted to letter-scale for posting to Hofstra’s final grade system online.
